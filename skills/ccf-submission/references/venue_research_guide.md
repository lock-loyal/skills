## Acceptance Rate Research

### Priority Sources

#### 1. Official Conference Statistics
Many conferences now publish acceptance rates on their websites.

**Where to look:**
- Conference homepage → "Statistics" or "About" section
- Conference handbook/program
- Opening/closing session slides (often posted online)

**Example URLs:**
- NeurIPS: Check neurips.cc under "Program" → "Statistics"
- ICML: Check icml.cc for acceptance statistics
- ACL: Check aclweb.org for conference statistics

#### 2. CSRankings
CSRankings (csrankings.org) provides conference metrics including approximate acceptance rates.

**Access method:**
- Navigate to csrankings.org
- Click on conference names to see metrics
- Filter by area (AI, Systems, Theory, etc.)

#### 3. OpenReview
For venues using OpenReview, statistics are often available.

**Venues using OpenReview:**
- ICLR (fully on OpenReview)
- NeurIPS (increasingly)
- ICML (partially)
- Some workshops

**How to find:**
- Go to openreview.net/group?id=[venue_id]
- Look for "Stats" or download data

#### 4. WikiCFP
WikiCFP maintains historical data on conferences.

**URL:** wikicfp.com/cfp/

**Search pattern:**
- Search for venue name
- Check historical CFPs for acceptance rate mentions

### Typical Acceptance Rate Ranges

| CCF Class | Typical Range | Notes |
|-----------|---------------|-------|
| CCF-A | 15-25% | Highly competitive |
| CCF-B | 20-35% | Moderately competitive |
| CCF-C | 25-45% | Less competitive |

**Exceptions exist** - Some A venues (like some journals) may have higher rates due to different review models.

### Research Tips

1. **Look for multi-year trends** - Single year rates can be anomalies
2. **Distinguish oral/poster** - Some conferences report combined rates
3. **Note review model changes** - Double-blind vs single-blind can affect rates
4. **Consider sub-area rates** - Some venues track by topic area

## Review Cycle Research

### Conference Timeline Structure

```
Submission → First Review → Author Response → Final Decision → Camera Ready → Conference
    |              |              |               |              |            |
  Month 0       Month 1-2      Month 2         Month 2-3      Month 3      Month 6-9
```

### Key Dates to Research

1. **Abstract Deadline** (if applicable)
2. **Paper Submission Deadline**
3. **Supplementary Material Deadline**
4. **Review Period**
5. **Author Response Period** (rebuttal)
6. **Notification Date**
7. **Camera-Ready Deadline**

### Where to Find Timeline Information

#### Official Sources (Most Reliable)
- Conference CFP page
- Conference website "Dates" section
- Twitter/X official conference accounts

#### Aggregation Sites
- WikiCFP (wikicfp.com)
- Conference Partner (conf.researchr.org)
- OpenResearch (openresearch.org)

### Typical Review Durations

| Venue Type | First Decision | Total Cycle |
|------------|----------------|-------------|
| Top AI Conf | 6-8 weeks | 2-3 months |
| Systems Conf | 8-12 weeks | 3-4 months |
| Theory Conf | 8-12 weeks | 3-4 months |
| Fast-track Journal | 1-2 months | 3-6 months |
| Regular Journal | 2-4 months | 6-12 months |

## Venue Characteristics Research

### Scope and Audience

**Key questions to answer:**
1. What topics does the venue prioritize?
2. What methodology is preferred? (theoretical, empirical, systems)
3. Who is the typical audience?
4. What's the typical paper length/format?

### Research Methods

#### 1. Recent Proceedings Analysis
- Download last 2-3 years of proceedings
- Analyze paper titles and topics
- Look at keynote topics

#### 2. Call for Papers (CFP)
- Check "Topics of Interest" section
- Note any special tracks or themes
- Check for "Call for Workshops"

#### 3. Social Media
- Follow conference Twitter/X accounts
- Check recent hashtag discussions
- Look at program committee announcements

#### 4. Community Discussions

**English sources:**
- Reddit r/MachineLearning
- Twitter/X academic community
- Paper Digest blog
- The Gradient (thegradient.pub)

**Chinese sources:**
- Zhihu "如何评价[venue]"
- CSDN venue analysis articles
- WeChat "[venue]投稿经验"

### Journal-Specific Factors

#### Impact Factor Sources
1. **Journal Citation Reports** (JCR) - Requires subscription
2. **Scopus** - CiteScore
3. **Google Scholar** - h5-index
4. **Journal website** - Often self-reports

#### Quality Indicators
- Editorial board reputation
- Turnaround time (faster ≠ better)
- Open access options and fees
- Special issue frequency

| Platform | Content Type | Reliability |
|----------|--------------|-------------|
| CSDN | Experience blogs | Medium-High |

| WeChat Articles | Analysis, summaries | Medium |
| 小红书 | Personal experiences | Medium |

### How to Evaluate Chinese Sources

1. **Check date** - Older posts may be outdated
2. **Look for specifics** - Vague claims less reliable
3. **Multiple sources** - Cross-reference experiences
4. **Author background** - Academic vs. practitioner
5. **Consistency** - Do multiple posts agree?

## International Sources

### CSRankings

**URL:** csrankings.org

**What it provides:**
- Conference metrics
- Faculty publication counts
- Venue rankings by area

**Limitations:**
- Limited to faculty publications
- May not reflect entire community
- Some areas underrepresented

### OpenReview

**URL:** openreview.net

**Venues covered:**
- ICLR (complete)
- NeurIPS workshops
- Some ICML tracks
- Various workshops

**Data available:**
- Acceptance rates (for public venues)

- Author response patterns

### Conference Websites

Always check the official website first for:
- Most accurate deadline information

## Research Checklist

When researching a venue, ensure you gather:

- [ ] CCF class and field classification
- [ ] Type (conference/journal)
- [ ] Acceptance rate (with source)
- [ ] Review timeline
- [ ] Next deadline (if conference)
- [ ] Scope and topics
- [ ] Recent trends or changes
- [ ] Comparison with similar venues

## Red Flags to Note

When researching, watch for:

1. **Rapidly declining acceptance rates** - Increasing competition
2. **Significant timeline changes** - May indicate organizational issues
3. **Scope drift** - Venue moving to different topics
4. **Predatory characteristics** - For unknown venues (unlikely in CCF)
5. **Outdated information** - Always check publication date
